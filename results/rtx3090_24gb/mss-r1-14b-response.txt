### 2. **Correlation Analysis**
   - **Autocorrelation**: Check if there's any correlation between prices at different lags. This can help identify patterns or cycles in the data.
   - **Cross-Correlation**: If you have multiple time series (e.g., different stocks), analyze how they correlate with each other.

### 3. **Fractal Analysis**
   - **Hurst Exponent**: Calculate the Hurst exponent to determine if the data exhibits long-term memory or persistence. This can help classify the data as either a random walk, mean-reverting, or trending.
   - **Self-Similarity**: Check for self-similar patterns at different time scales, which is a characteristic of fractals.

### 4. **Nonlinear Dynamics**
   - **Recurrence Plots**: These plots can help visualize the recurrence of states in the system, which might reveal underlying dynamics.
   - **Lyapunov Exponents**: Calculate these to determine the predictability of the system. Positive Lyapunov exponents indicate chaos and sensitivity to initial conditions.

### 7. **Wavelet Analysis**
